Rochester Rally


The Rochester Rally is a practice competition hosted by Penfield High School. All teams in the area are invited, and this year, they had record attendance – nearly 20 teams came to the Rally.

The day starts out with simple practice on the mock field, a chance for teams to test out their robots in the arena. The drivers get their first experience driving the robot on the field; shooting frisbees into goals, putting new frisbees into the robot at the feeder stations, and climbing on the metal pyramids.

Later, scrimmage matches are scheduled, and any team can enter a match when they’re ready.  Other teams scramble to take note of which robots can shoot well or climb fast, already strategizing for matches in the real competition.

In the pits this morning, the Panthers raced to attach our climbing mechanism to the robot. Once we were finally ready, we jumped on the chance for a practice match, and brought our robot out to the field – only to have our netbook run out of battery! Oops!

Once that situation was remedied, the Panthers’ drive team shot frisbees clear across the field. The robot was able to score only feet away from the loading area. Loading frisbees was a little messy, because our robot doesn’t have protective shields around it yet, but our robot was able to drive over the frisbees that landed on the ground. Near the end of one of the matches, our human player Eddie was able to throw a blue Frisbee into the pyramid’s goal. Some of the mentors hefted the robot during a practice intermission to test out the climbing mechanism with minimal damage.

We headed out at 4:30, the Rally chalked up to a great success. We were able to test our robot and work out some kinks, and everyone had fun.
